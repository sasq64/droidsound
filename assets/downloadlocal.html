How To Download

To import a database or music pack into Droidound, you have a couple of options;

  • Download the zip-file directly from here using your phone, and click on it once it's been downloaded by the download manager. It should either be imported directly by Droidsound, or you will get a dialog indicating which program should handle the file. In the latter case (obviously) select Droidsound.
  • Download the file to your PC, and copy it directly into your SD cards MODS/ directory (either over USB or with a dedicated sdcard reader). It should automatically be scanned next time you start Droidsound.
  • Also, a well-behaved file manager (on your phone) should respect the configured MIME types and also let Droidsound open zip-files. This is not always the case though (for instance, Galaxy S built-in manager fails at this).

About

HVSC

http://www.hvsc.de/

The High Voltage Sid Collection contains more or less every C64 tune ever released and is a must for anyone who likes the sound of SID.

Atari SAP Music Collection (ASMA)

http://www.asma.atari.org/

The one and only official Atari XL/XE music archive, also the biggest. If you loved the Atari 8-bit family, then this is for you.

CSDb Database

http://noname.c64.org/csdb/

CSDb is the premier site for the C64 Demo scene, and is the place to go to download demos, music or artwork for the C64.

This database dump contains every release that references SID music from HVSC (above) and allows you to browse for music by Party, Group or Top Releases

ym rockerz

Demovibes

http://www.demovibes.org/

Demovibes is a mixed compilation of the finest demoscene electronic music. Designed to fit each volume on 80 minutes Audio CDs, its aim is to reach new audiences, external from demoscene.
